# Nietzsche Profile — Genealogy of Concepts

> **Aesthetic:** Philosophical/genealogical mapping via concept hierarchies. 256-color palette, dialectical structures. Visualizes the "genealogy" of values, concepts, and power: how ideas emerge, transform, oppose, and synthesize.

The **nietzsche** profile generates images representing Nietzschean philosophy: the genealogy and becoming of concepts, the dialectic of opposition (Apollonian/Dionysian, slave morality/master morality), eternal recurrence, and the Übermensch. Visual metaphors use **hierarchies, opposing forces, cycles, and transformations** to show how values and concepts evolve.

---

## Competency Questions (Scope)

1. **Genealogy:** "What is the historical origin and transformation of this concept?"
2. **Apollonian vs Dionysian:** "Is this concept more structured (Apollonian) or chaotic (Dionysian)?"
3. **Master vs Slave Morality:** "What values underlie this concept? Power-affirming or ressentiment?"
4. **Eternal Recurrence:** "Can I visualize this concept as a cycle that repeats eternally?"
5. **Concept Hierarchy:** "What concepts are 'parents' or 'children' of this one? How do they relate?"

## Conceptual Framework: Nietzschean Philosophy

### Fundamental Dialectics

#### 1. Apollonian (Order) vs Dionysian (Chaos)

```
APOLLONIAN                          DIONYSIAN
├─ Form, order, rationality         ├─ Formlessness, chaos, passion
├─ Individual, boundary              ├─ Collective, dissolution
├─ Light, clarity, appearance        ├─ Darkness, mystery, ecstasy
├─ Dream, illusion of form           ├─ Intoxication, raw experience
├─ Apollonian art: sculpture, plot   ├─ Dionysian art: music, dance
└─ Sun god (distant, perfect)        └─ God of wine (intimate, messy)

Neither is "better"; both necessary for art, culture, life.
Goal: balance and creative tension.
```

#### 2. Master Morality vs Slave Morality

```
MASTER MORALITY                     SLAVE MORALITY
├─ Good = power, life-affirming     ├─ Good = weakness, humility
├─ Evil = weakness, life-negating   ├─ Evil = power, pride (ressentiment)
├─ Creates values (active)          ├─ Reacts against (reactive)
├─ Asserts strength, nobility       ├─ Asserts victimhood, suffering
├─ Life-affirming                   ├─ Life-negating (sublimated hatred)
└─ Example: warrior, artist         └─ Example: priest, monk, victim

Genealogy: Slave morality emerged when the weak defeated the strong
via moral inversion. Christianity = inverted morality.
```

#### 3. Becoming (Flux, Growth, Transformation)

```
Not Being (static essence) but Becoming (eternal change)
    Nothing is fixed
    Everything flows and transforms
    Concepts evolve through struggle
    Values emerge from will-to-power

Visual: Spirals, arrows, cycles, metamorphosis, flames
Not: Statues, completed forms, eternal truths
```

#### 4. The Eternal Recurrence (Amor Fati)

```
Imagine: Everything that has happened will happen again eternally.
Question: Could you affirm your life if it had to repeat forever?

Visual: Cycles, spirals returning, wheels, seasonal patterns
Philosophy: The ultimate test of life-affirmation
Nietzsche's answer: Yes, if you become the Übermensch
```

#### 5. The Übermensch (Beyond-Man)

```
The human who:
├─ Creates their own values (not follows herd morality)
├─ Affirms life despite suffering (yes-saying to existence)
├─ Embodies both Apollonian form AND Dionysian passion
├─ Wills their own becoming eternally
└─ Goes "beyond good and evil" (beyond slave/master dichotomy)

Not: Superman, tyrant, or übermensch-as-elite
But: Individual who creates meaning in a meaningless cosmos
Visual: Ascending, transforming, dancing, creative fire
```

## Generation Prompt Template

```
Generate SVG for the Nietzsche profile.

Concepts: [user tags]
Genealogical focus: [optional: will-to-power, eternal-recurrence, master-morality, slave-morality, Übermensch]
Dialectic: [optional: Apollonian-Dionysian, ascending-descending, creation-destruction]
Affirmative stance: [life-affirming or life-negating?]

Constraints:
- Max 80 elements (can be dense)
- Palette: 256-color (full range, use opposing colors)
- Show genealogy: origin → transformation → becoming
- Visualize dialectical tension (not resolution, but creative struggle)
- Consider eternal recurrence: could this image repeat forever?

Genealogical Guidelines:
- Ascending forms (golden, geometric) for master-morality / life-affirmation / Apollonian
- Descending forms (gray, inverted) for slave-morality / life-negation / reactive forces
- Spirals and cycles for eternal recurrence, becoming, will-to-power
- Integration (both Apollonian and Dionysian) for Übermensch or tragedy

Tone: philosophical, dialectical, affirmative yet tragic, genealogical
Example: If concepts are ['will-to-power', 'eternal-recurrence', 'Übermensch']:
  - Center: ascending spiral (will-to-power)
  - Surrounding: cycle (eternal recurrence)
  - Top: figure (Übermensch) dancing/creating
  - Integration: golden and red, organized yet wild

Output: valid SVG
```

```

                 ──opposes──> slave-morality
                 ──creates──> hierarchy, values, art

               ──opposes──> master-morality
               ──creates──> inverted values, guilt, denial

           ──represents──> form, order, individuation

           ──represents──> chaos, ecstasy, dissolution

         ──historical-form──> Greek tragedy
         ──modern-form──> lost, attempted in opera

            ──integrates──> Apollonian + Dionysian

            ──dances──> life affirmation
```
